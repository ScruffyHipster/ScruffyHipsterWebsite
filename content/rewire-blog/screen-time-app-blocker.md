A Screen Time app blocker uses Apple's iOS controls to restrict selected apps and websites. That is the right foundation for iPhone blocking because iOS does not let third-party apps take unlimited control over other apps.

This is a good thing. Apple's safeguards help keep blocking honest. A blocker can help you create friction, but it should not claim to bypass the system or remove every possible way around a restriction.

## what Screen Time based blockers can do

- Let you choose apps to block
- Include distracting websites
- Run timed or always-on sessions
- Use Apple's Family Controls permissions
- Show simple patterns around blocked attempts or sessions

The exact behavior depends on the app and the current version of iOS.

## why this matters

If an app blocker is honest about iOS limits, you can trust the product framing more. Look for specific language around Screen Time, privacy, and what the app does when you open something blocked.
