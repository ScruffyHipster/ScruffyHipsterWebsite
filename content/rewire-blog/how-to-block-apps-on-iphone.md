To block apps on iPhone, you can use Apple's built-in Screen Time controls or an app blocker that works with Screen Time. The basic pattern is the same: choose the apps that distract you, decide when blocking should run, and make access harder during that window.

Apple's built-in route is useful when you want simple limits. Open Settings, go to Screen Time, and use App Limits or Downtime to restrict categories or specific apps. This works well for broad limits, especially if you want something already built into iOS.

## when a dedicated blocker helps

A dedicated app blocker helps when you want faster focus sessions, app and website lists that match different parts of your day, or an interruption that makes the reflex visible before the app opens.

That matters because many distracting opens are not planned. You pick up the phone for one thing, tap a familiar icon, and only notice the detour later.

## where rewire fits

[Rewire](/rewire) is an iPhone app blocker built around that moment. You choose apps and websites, start a timed or always-on focus session, and Rewire uses Apple's Screen Time controls to add friction when you try to open something blocked.

Rewire does not bypass Apple's protections. It works with them. That keeps the promise realistic: useful friction, not impossible control over the operating system.

## a simple setup

1. Pick three to five apps that most often interrupt you.
2. Add websites if the same distraction moves into Safari.
3. Start a short focus session.
4. Review what you tried to open and adjust the list.

Start small. A blocker works best when it matches the real habit rather than a perfect version of your day.
